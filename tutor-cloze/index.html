The Hidden Concerns of "Sharenting" on Social Media

4 Questions
While the term “sharenting” was coined as recently as 2010, it has become a worldwide phenomenon. According to the Collins Dictionary, sharenting is “the habitual use of social media to share news, images, and other private data about one’s children.”
Sharenting may start from the ultrasound images of a baby to the mom’s life during pregnancy and continue after the child is born. Information on children, such as gender, time and place of birth, photographs, and other digital information is put on social media and shared by parents and relatives.
However, the simple act of parents sharing their children’s photos and activities has put them in danger.

The most obvious peril of sharenting is crime against children. Revealing an excessive amount of information, especially identification information, may lead to digital kidnapping when users of social media steal a picture or video and repost it as their own child’s. Also, it can harm a child’s future financial security and make them a future target of scams.
The other concern is the psychological effect. Many children report feeling ashamed the first time they go online, usually as tweens or teenagers, to see their entire life online.
In extreme cases, cyberbullying caused by sharenting can take its toll on a child’s mental health. So, can parents still have the joy of sharenting? The answer is yes, as long as parents can “share with care.”
